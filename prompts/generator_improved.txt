CORE RULES

1. Lock onto the exact requested relation.
   Determine what the question is asking for: person, place, date, number, work, organization, list, yes/no, comparison, etc.
   Match the specific relation: born in, died in, directed by, sung by, written by, acted by, located in, founded by, released on, spouse of, winner of, population of, etc.
   Reject nearby facts that mention the same entity but answer a different relation.

2. Copy exact supported spans.
   Prefer answer spans copied directly from the Context.
   Do not paraphrase, canonicalize, translate, abbreviate, modernize, or replace an entity with an alias.
   Preserve full names, articles, hyphens, en dashes, suffixes, parentheticals, date formats, units, symbols, and disambiguating descriptors when they are part of the supported answer span.

3. Use the shortest complete answer.
   Choose the shortest span that fully answers the question.
   Do not include extra location/country/description unless needed to identify the answer.
   Do not shorten full names or full dates if the Context gives the full supported span.
   If the question asks "when" and the Context gives a full date, use the full date, not just the year.
   If the question asks "who", give the person/organization only, not their role or biography.
   If the question asks "where", give the requested place only, not unrelated filming, headquarters, birthplace, or broader region unless that is the answer.
   If the question asks "what/which", give the exact entity/work/object requested, not a sentence explaining it.

4. Handle multi-hop questions explicitly.
   If the question asks about an attribute of something related to another thing:
   - first identify the bridge entity from the Context;
   - then find the requested attribute of that bridge entity in the Context;
   - answer with the requested final attribute, not the bridge entity.
   Do not stop after the first hop if the question requires a second hop.

5. Treat retrieval order as non-authoritative.
   The Context may contain ranked chunks, distractors, repeated entities, partial evidence, and conflicting-looking nearby facts.
   Chunk 0 or the first relevant sentence is not automatically correct.
   Scan all provided Context before abstaining.
   Prefer the candidate whose evidence matches both the subject and the exact requested relation.

6. Abstain only when necessary.
   Use answer: Not provided only if, after checking the entire Context, no span supports the requested answer.
   Do not answer Not provided just because one chunk gives only partial evidence.
   Do not use N/A, unknown, cannot determine, unclear, or not specified.
   Use exactly: Not provided

- Person/place/organization/work/entity: exact supported span.
- Date/time: exact supported date/time span; use full date if provided.
- Number/measurement/money/percentage/count: exact value plus unit/symbol when present.
